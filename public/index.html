How to pack for International Travel

May 18, 2019

I've spent the last two days frantically doing everything that's optional and nothing that's necessary. I've rock climbed whenever I wanted to, I played five games of pool last night, I drank Barefoot and cleaned my entire house. It's been a nice week.

That brings me to the present moment. I'm splayed out over two benches in the arrivals section of the Raleigh-Durham International airport, my packed bags littered around my lounging self. I'm going to Copenhagen for a study abroad program today, and I have to build a website before I get there. Hah. I wonder how long that'll take. I'm not stressed. I should emphasize the fact that I really am lounging in this Starbucks. Read this: socks and sandals, baby. It's 2019. I don't have time for pinched pinky toes.

A fanny pack. Laugh, yes, but please picture yourself rummaging through your cavernous pockets for your boarding pass, passport, iPhone charger, iPhone, mint gum; your cargo shorts weighed down to the floor in a way that would make Antoine Dodson smile, a sad state of affairs. You can see the future. Make of that how you will. Now picture me; tribal patterned fanny pack at-waist, posted up at the front of the boarding line; wielding my documents with finesse. This is how you pack and prepare for international travel.

You man wishes that he bought his suitcase for four dollars. Your man wishes he was living off Pop-Chips and RxBars. International Travel baby, he's the most International Man in The World. Thank you. Welcome to my #blog.
